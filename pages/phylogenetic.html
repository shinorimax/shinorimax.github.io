Research Summary

I am currently working as a research assitant at the Palacios Lab, under the supervision of Prof. Julia Palacios. My research focuses on detecting selection on a chromosome by applying a distance metric that captures both tree topology and branch length, features not incorporated in traditional methods. I simulate genealogical trees using Python and msprime, generating neutral and selected scenarios for analysis. Statistical analysis is conducted in R to evaluate how well the distance metric differentiates between selection and neutrality. By leveraging tree structure and branch length, our approach aims to provide a more nuanced detection method compared to site-frequency-based statistics.
