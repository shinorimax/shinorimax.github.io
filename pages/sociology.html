Research Summary

I worked as a research assistant under Professor Kiyoteru Tsutsui in the Sociology Department at Stanford. Using Python and NLP libraries such as Scikit-learn and MeCab, I conducted textual analysis of Japan’s “Basic Policy on Economic and Fiscal Management and Reform” documents from the past two decades to uncover key policy trends. I also assembled and visualized the Varieties of Democracy (V-Dem) index for over 200 countries across 100 years using R, contributing to a published report in Nikkei, the world’s largest financial newspaper. Additionally, I generated over 200 visualizations and translated 100+ survey items for the Stanford Japan Barometer, a social survey capturing public opinion on major political, economic, and social issues in Japan. My work enabled cross-national and longitudinal analysis, supporting both academic research and public-facing insights.
