Project Summary

This project investigates whether batting average (hit frequency) contributes to offensive consistency in run production, challenging the conventional devaluation of the metric in modern baseball analytics. To quantify consistency, I built a Markov chain model in R with a 25 by 25 transition probability matrix, simulating inning outcomes based on different [outs, runners on base] configurations using 2023 MLB play-by-play data from Retrosheet. By running simulations for various players, we measured variance in runs produced and the probability of producing at least X runs, comparing high-batting-average contact hitters to low-average power hitters. Results showed that hit frequency plays a crucial role in producing at least one or two runs, particularly in high-leverage situations, while home runs drive higher expected runs but with greater variance
